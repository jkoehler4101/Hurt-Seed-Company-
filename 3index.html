General History

  1. In 1932, Fred Hurt opened Hurt Seed Company in Halls, Tn.
  2. Throughout the 1950s, Hurt Seed Company started processing soybeans.
  3. In the 1960s Fred Hurt retired and passed buisness on to son, David Hurt Sr.Around this time David Hurt Jr. joined the family buisness.
  4. Through the 1970s the company was expanded and sold wholesale, certified, public soybeans.
  5. In 1985, the buisness was passed down to David Hurt III. In 1992 Raymond Hurt joined the company and co-owned the buisness with his brother, David Hurt III.
  6. After this, Asgrow beagn to condition their soybeans, in 1995, through an alliance program.
  7. Since Hurt Seed started conditoning soybeans, they bulit a conditioning plant in 1998, capable of processing 750,000 units a year, and then added a Thiele heat-seal bagger.
  8. In 2002, Hurt Seed built their current office location.
  9. Hurt Seed made major plant renovations in 2007, adding all-new, larger conditioning equipment, doubling capacity.
  10. In 2009 a 100,000 bushel bulk storage capcity and a 2nd dump pit were added.
  11. In 2010 the cleaning plant was renovated, adding a mini-bulk bagger, new color sorters, and automation to track seed from dumping to packaging. Hurt Seed also sold their retail busisness in 2010 to focus mainly on production.
  12. In 2011 a new treatment system with 2nd heat seal bagger ,to meet Monsanto Acceleron needs, was added.
  13. 25,000 bushel bulk storage added in 2012.
  14. In 2013 Hurt Seed purchased a 120,000 sq.ft. warehouse for storage and loading purposes.
  15. In 2015 a TruBulk load-out facility was added.
  16. In 2017 rotary spirals were addded and the production plant was upgraded.
